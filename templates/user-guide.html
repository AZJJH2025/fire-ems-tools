Introduction to FireEMS.ai

What is FireEMS.ai?

FireEMS.ai is a comprehensive suite of analytics and management tools designed specifically for Fire and EMS agencies. Our platform helps you visualize incident data, optimize response times, manage resources, and make data-driven decisions to improve emergency services.

Whether you're a small volunteer department or a large metropolitan agency, FireEMS.ai scales to meet your needs with powerful tools that are intuitive and easy to use.

Key Features

FireEMS.ai includes the following core features:

  • Incident Logger - A comprehensive incident reporting system with HIPAA compliance and NFIRS integration
  • Response Time Analyzer - Analyze response time patterns to identify areas for improvement
  • Call Density Heatmap - Visualize incident hotspots to optimize resource deployment
  • Isochrone Map - Map response time coverage to identify service gaps
  • Station Overview - Monitor performance metrics for individual stations
  • Coverage Gap Finder - Identify underserved areas in your jurisdiction
  • FireMapPro - Advanced interactive mapping tools for planning
  • Data Formatter - Prepare and standardize your data for analysis

System Requirements

FireEMS.ai is a web-based platform that works on any modern browser. For optimal performance, we recommend:

  • Browsers: Chrome (recommended), Firefox, Safari, or Edge (latest versions)
  • Internet Connection: Broadband connection (minimum 5 Mbps)
  • Screen Resolution: Minimum 1280 x 800
  • Mobile Devices: iOS 13+ or Android 10+

Tip: For the best experience with mapping features, we recommend using a device with at least 8GB of RAM.

Account Setup

Registration Process

To get started with FireEMS.ai, your department administrator needs to register your agency:

  1. Visit the FireEMS.ai homepage and click "Register Department"
  2. Fill out the department registration form with your agency information
  3. Select the subscription plan that fits your needs
  4. Complete the payment process
  5. Verify your email address to activate your account

Note: The person who completes registration will automatically become the initial department administrator with full access to all features.

Department Profile Setup

After registration, complete your department profile:

  1. Upload your department logo
  2. Configure department settings (time zone, units of measurement, etc.)
  3. Set up initial stations and resources
  4. Add users and assign roles
  5. Import historical incident data (optional)

Tip: Use the Setup Wizard for a guided step-by-step process to complete your department profile setup.

User Account Setup

Once invited to join by your administrator, follow these steps to set up your user account:

  1. Check your email for the invitation link
  2. Create a secure password (minimum 8 characters with letters, numbers, and special characters)
  3. Complete your user profile information
  4. Review and accept the terms of service
  5. Complete the brief orientation tutorial

Important: For security reasons, invitation links expire after 48 hours. If your link has expired, contact your department administrator for a new invitation.

Incident Logger

Creating Incidents

The Incident Logger is your primary tool for recording emergency incident information:

  1. Navigate to the Incident Logger from the main menu
  2. Click the "New Incident" button in the top right corner
  3. Complete the required incident information:
    • Incident number (generated automatically or enter manually)
    • Date and time
    • Location (address or coordinates)
    • Incident type
    • Units responding
  4. Add additional details as needed (see sections below)
  5. Click "Save Incident" to create the record

Note: You can save an incident as "Draft" if you need to complete it later.

Working with Medical Sections

For EMS incidents, you can record detailed patient information:

  1. In the incident form, click "Add Patient" to create a new patient record
  2. Enter patient demographic information
  3. Record vital signs (initial and ongoing)
  4. Document patient history:
    • Medical history
    • Current medications
    • Allergies
  5. Document assessment findings
  6. Record treatments provided
  7. Note transport information

Important: All patient information is protected under HIPAA. Access is logged and restricted to authorized users only.

NFIRS Reporting

FireEMS.ai supports National Fire Incident Reporting System (NFIRS) reporting:

  1. Complete the incident information as described above
  2. Navigate to the NFIRS tab within the incident
  3. Complete all required NFIRS fields:
    • Basic incident information (Module 1)
    • Fire information (Module 2, if applicable)
    • Structural fire information (Module 3, if applicable)
    • Civilian fire casualty information (Module 4, if applicable)
    • Fire service casualty information (Module 5, if applicable)
    • EMS information (Module 6, if applicable)
    • Hazardous materials information (Module 7, if applicable)
    • Wildland fire information (Module 8, if applicable)
    • Apparatus and personnel information (Module 9, if applicable)
    • Additional specialized information (Module 10+, if applicable)
  4. Use the validation tool to check for errors or missing information
  5. Generate the NFIRS export file for submission

Tip: The system will automatically populate many NFIRS fields based on the information you've already entered in the main incident form.

FireMapPro

Overview

FireMapPro is an advanced interactive mapping tool designed specifically for fire and EMS agencies. It allows you to create professional maps for planning, operations, and reporting purposes with customizable features and export options.

Note: FireMapPro combines the power of Leaflet mapping with customized tools for fire service applications, allowing you to create detailed operational maps and export them in various formats.

Key Features

FireMapPro includes the following capabilities:

  • Interactive Maps - Create, customize, and interact with detailed maps
  • Multiple Map Layers - Switch between street, satellite, and terrain views
  • Drag-and-Drop Icons - Add fire apparatus, EMS units, incident types, and other markers
  • Drawing Tools - Add shapes, lines, and annotations to your maps
  • Enhanced Measurement Tools - Measure distances, areas, and perimeters with customizable units
  • Layout Designer - Create professional map layouts with titles, legends, and scale bars
  • Export Options - Export maps in various formats (PNG, JPG, PDF)
  • GIS Data Support - Import and visualize GIS data (CSV, GeoJSON, KML)
  • Analysis Tools - Create heatmaps, buffer zones, and perform spatial analysis

Recent Updates

The latest version of FireMapPro includes several key improvements:

  • Enhanced Map Layers - Improved terrain layer with OpenTopoMap for better topographic visualization
  • Consistent Icon Coloring - All map icons now display with proper coloring and visibility
  • Improved Export Functionality - More reliable exports with enhanced compatibility across browsers
  • Layout Designer Enhancements - Better drag-and-drop functionality and element positioning
  • Performance Optimization - Faster map loading and smoother interaction, especially with complex maps

Tip: The terrain layer is particularly useful for wildland fire planning and areas with significant elevation changes.

Basic Map Controls

Navigate and control the map using these tools:

  • Pan - Click and drag to move the map
  • Zoom - Use the + and - buttons or mouse wheel to zoom in/out
  • Layer Control - Select different base maps (street, satellite, terrain)
  • Full Screen - Expand the map to fill the entire screen
  • Reset View - Return to the default map view
  • Coordinates Display - Shows current mouse coordinates

Adding Map Elements

Enhance your map with various elements:

  1. Icons and Symbols:
    • Locate icons in the sidebar categories (Fire Apparatus, EMS Units, etc.)
    • Drag and drop icons onto the map
    • Icons will appear at the drop location and can be moved by dragging
    • Click on an icon to display a popup with options
    • Use the delete button in the popup to remove icons
  2. Drawing Tools:
    • Click the "Draw Tools" button to display the drawing toolbar
    • Use tools to add points, lines, polygons, rectangles, and circles
    • Edit existing shapes by selecting them and using the edit tools
    • Delete shapes by selecting them and using the delete tool
  3. Enhanced Measurement Tool:
    • Click the "Measure" button to activate measurement mode
    • Choose between measuring distance, area, or perimeter
    • Select your preferred measurement units (feet, meters, miles, kilometers)
    • Click points on the map to create measurement lines or shapes
    • Complete a measurement by double-clicking
    • View measurements directly on the map with clear labels
  4. Map Title:
    • Click the title toggle button to show/hide the map title banner
    • Click the edit button to customize the title and subtitle
    • Add a department logo to the title banner

Analysis Tools

Perform advanced spatial analysis:

  • Hotspot Analysis:
    • Click the "Hotspot Analysis" button to open the analysis panel
    • Select the data layer to analyze
    • Set the analysis radius and intensity
    • Generate a heatmap showing incident density
  • Distance Analysis:
    • Click the "Distance Analysis" button to measure between points
    • Add points by clicking on the map
    • View distances between consecutive points
    • See the total route distance
  • Buffer Tool:
    • Click the "Create Buffer" button to open the buffer panel
    • Set the buffer distance in miles
    • Select which layers to apply the buffer to
    • Generate buffer zones around the selected points

Importing Data

Add your own data to the map:

  1. CSV Import:
    • Click "Import CSV" in the sidebar
    • Select a CSV file with latitude and longitude data
    • Map the appropriate columns in the CSV mapping modal
    • Select an icon style for the imported points
    • Click "Import Data" to add the points to the map
  2. KML/GPX Import:
    • Click "Import KML/GPX" in the sidebar
    • Select a KML or GPX file
    • The data will be automatically added to the map
  3. GeoJSON Import:
    • Click "Import GeoJSON" in the sidebar
    • Select a GeoJSON file
    • The data will be automatically added to the map

Tip: When importing CSV data, ensure your coordinate columns are clearly labeled (e.g., "latitude", "longitude") for automatic detection.

Exporting Maps

Create professional exports of your maps using these steps:

  1. Click one of the export buttons in the sidebar:
    • "Export PNG" - For raster image export
    • "Export PDF" - For document-based export
    • "Export Data" - For GIS data export
  2. In the export modal, choose between three export modes:
    • Basic - Quick export with simple options
    • Advanced - Detailed control over export settings
    • Layout Designer - Create professional map layouts
  3. Configure your export settings:
    • Title and subtitle
    • Export format (PNG, JPG, PDF, etc.)
    • Resolution (DPI)
    • Paper size and orientation
    • Element inclusion (legend, scale, north arrow)
  4. Click "Export Map" to generate your export
  5. Review the print preview before final export
  6. Download the exported file to your device

Note: Higher resolution exports (300+ DPI) will result in larger file sizes but better print quality.

Tip: The improved export functionality now ensures that all map elements and colors are accurately preserved in the exported files.

Using the Layout Designer

Create professional map layouts for printing and presentations:

  1. In the export modal, click the "Layout Designer" tab
  2. Select a template (Standard, Professional, Presentation, or Tactical)
  3. Choose the page orientation (portrait or landscape)
  4. Customize layout elements:
    • Drag elements to reposition them
    • Add new elements from the left sidebar
    • Remove elements by clicking the X button
    • Edit text content by selecting an element and editing in the properties panel
    • Upload images to image elements using the upload button
  5. Preview your layout in the print preview
  6. Export the final layout as PDF, PNG, or JPG

Tip: For detailed maps with specific size requirements, use the Advanced export tab to set exact dimensions and resolution before creating your layout.

Map Layers

FireMapPro offers several base map layers for different visualization needs:

  • OpenStreetMap - Default street map showing roads, buildings, and infrastructure
  • Satellite - High-resolution aerial imagery for detailed ground view
  • Terrain - Enhanced topographic view showing elevation changes and natural features using OpenTopoMap
  • Esri World Imagery - Alternative satellite view with global coverage
  • Dark Mode - Low-light map for nighttime operations or reduced screen glare

To switch between layers:

  1. Click the layers icon in the top-right corner of the map
  2. Select your preferred base layer from the dropdown
  3. Optional overlay layers can be toggled on/off using the checkboxes

Note: The terrain layer has been significantly improved in the latest update, with better detail and reliability.

Best Practices

Follow these tips for the best results when using FireMapPro:

  • Use appropriate icon sizes to avoid cluttering the map
  • Organize information by using different layers
  • Include a legend to explain map symbology
  • Add a scale bar for proper distance perspective
  • Include a north arrow for orientation
  • Use the terrain layer for topographic features
  • Create buffer zones to show coverage areas
  • Save complex maps frequently by exporting them
  • For printing, use at least 300 DPI resolution
  • Test exports at smaller sizes before creating large-format prints
  • Use the enhanced measurement tools for accurate distance and area calculations

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Solutions for common problems you might encounter:

  • Map Not Loading Properly:
    • Try refreshing the page
    • Check your internet connection
    • Clear your browser cache
    • Try a different browser (Chrome recommended)
  • Export Fails or Looks Incorrect:
    • Ensure all map elements are within the visible area
    • Try reducing the map complexity if exporting a large area
    • For complex maps, try exporting in parts
    • Use the PNG format for best quality
  • Drawing Tools Not Working:
    • Make sure you've activated the drawing mode first
    • Try deselecting any currently selected elements
    • Close and reopen the drawing toolbar
  • Imported Data Not Appearing:
    • Verify your data has proper coordinate information
    • Check that coordinates are in decimal degrees format
    • Try zooming out to see if the data is outside your current view

Data Formatter

Overview

The Data Formatter is a powerful tool that helps you transform, standardize, and prepare your data files for use with any FireEMS.ai tool. It automatically maps fields, converts formats, and ensures your data is ready for analysis without the need for manual preprocessing.

Note: Data Formatter supports a wide range of input formats including CSV, Excel, JSON, XML, and KML/KMZ files, and can recognize data from most common CAD/RMS systems used in the fire and EMS service.

Key Features

The Data Formatter includes the following capabilities:

  • File Format Conversion - Convert between different file formats (CSV, Excel, JSON, etc.)
  • Automatic Field Mapping - Smart detection of field names and data types
  • CAD/RMS System Detection - Automatic recognition of common fire service software exports
  • Data Cleaning - Standardization of dates, times, addresses, and coordinates
  • Missing Value Handling - Options to remove, fill, or interpolate missing data
  • Data Reduction - Compression options to reduce file size while preserving important information
  • Tool-Specific Formatting - Tailored data preparation for each FireEMS.ai tool
  • Direct Tool Integration - Send formatted data directly to your selected tool

Using the Data Formatter

Follow these steps to format your data:

  1. Upload your file:
    • Click "Choose File" in the Input Data panel
    • Select a CSV, Excel, JSON, XML, or KML/KMZ file from your computer
    • If uploading an Excel file, select the specific sheet to use
  2. Select your target tool:
    • Choose which FireEMS.ai tool you want to prepare the data for
    • Required fields for the selected tool will be automatically highlighted
  3. Set transformation options (optional):
    • Expand the "Advanced Options" section
    • Configure date formats, compression level, and how to handle missing values
    • Enable/disable duplicate removal and address standardization
  4. Transform the data:
    • Click the "Transform Data" button
    • Watch the transformation log for progress updates
  5. Review the output:
    • Check the output preview to ensure data looks correct
    • Select your preferred output format (CSV, Excel, or JSON)
  6. Use the formatted data:
    • Click "Download" to save the formatted data to your computer
    • Or click "Send to Tool" to directly open your selected tool with the formatted data

Supported Data Sources

The Data Formatter can automatically detect and properly format data from these common fire service systems:

  • Emergency Reporting - NFIRS-compliant format
  • ESO FireRMS - Medical/Fire hybrid format
  • ImageTrend - EMS/Fire Records Management System
  • Spillman Flex (Motorola) - Law enforcement integration
  • CentralSquare Small Agency - Simplified exports
  • Alpine Software/RedNMX - Rural department focused
  • Regional Shared Systems - Standardized formats
  • Central Square CAD - Standard format
  • Motorola/PremierOne CAD
  • Tyler New World CAD
  • Hexagon/Intergraph CAD

Tip: If your CAD/RMS system isn't listed, you can still use the Data Formatter. The tool will attempt to map fields based on common naming patterns and data structures.

Tool-Specific Data Requirements

Each FireEMS.ai tool has specific data requirements. Here are the key fields needed for major tools:

Response Time Analyzer

  • Incident ID
  • Incident Date
  • Incident Time
  • Dispatch Time
  • En Route Time
  • On Scene Time
  • Incident Type
  • Latitude
  • Longitude

Call Density Heatmap

  • Incident ID
  • Incident Date
  • Incident Time
  • Latitude
  • Longitude
  • Incident Type (optional)

Isochrone Map

  • Station ID
  • Station Name
  • Station Address
  • Latitude
  • Longitude
  • Unit Types

Note: When you select a target tool, the Data Formatter will highlight which required fields have been identified in your data and which ones are missing.

Advanced Options

The Data Formatter provides several advanced options for customizing the transformation process:

Date Format

Specify how dates in your source data are formatted:

  • Auto-detect - The tool will try to determine date formats automatically
  • MM/DD/YYYY - U.S. format (e.g., 01/31/2025)
  • DD/MM/YYYY - European format (e.g., 31/01/2025)
  • YYYY-MM-DD - ISO format (e.g., 2025-01-31)
  • Custom - Define your own date format pattern

Compression

Reduce file size while preserving important information:

  • None - No compression, preserve all data
  • Light - Remove unnecessary columns and reduce precision slightly
  • Medium - Combine similar entries and reduce precision
  • High - Maximum compression, may lose some detail

Missing Value Handling

Choose how to handle missing data:

  • Remove rows - Delete entries with missing required fields
  • Fill with defaults - Insert placeholder values
  • Interpolate - Calculate missing values based on surrounding data when possible

Transformation Examples

Example 1: Preparing Incident Data for Response Time Analyzer

Input: CAD export with incident details and timestamps

Transformations:

  • Converts timestamps to a consistent format
  • Breaks down single datetime fields into separate date/time fields
  • Geocodes addresses to latitude/longitude if coordinates are missing
  • Standardizes incident types to match system categories

Example 2: Formatting Data for Call Density Heatmap

Input: Excel file with call history

Transformations:

  • Extracts location data from address fields
  • Converts addresses to coordinates if needed
  • Normalizes incident types for filtering
  • Removes duplicate entries for the same incident

Example 3: Preparing Station Data for Isochrone Map

Input: Department station roster in CSV format

Transformations:

  • Standardizes station addresses
  • Converts addresses to precise coordinates
  • Categorizes apparatus types by standard classes
  • Creates a properly structured station dataset

Tips for Best Results

  • Use files with column headers/field names when possible
  • Review the transformation log to understand what changes were made
  • For Excel files with multiple sheets, choose the specific sheet containing your data
  • Try different compression levels if you have very large datasets
  • Save your formatted data for reuse with different tools
  • For address data, ensure the original addresses are as complete as possible
  • When possible, include explicit latitude/longitude data rather than relying on geocoding

Tip: For very large datasets (over 100,000 records), consider breaking the data into smaller chunks or using the high compression setting.

Coverage Gap Finder

Overview

The Coverage Gap Finder is a sophisticated analytical tool designed to help fire and EMS agencies identify underserved areas within their jurisdiction. This tool combines geospatial analysis with incident data to optimize station locations and resource deployment, ensuring faster response times and better service delivery.

Note: The Coverage Gap Finder uses advanced spatial algorithms to analyze response capabilities based on your existing station locations, incident patterns, and population distribution.

Key Features

The Coverage Gap Finder includes the following capabilities:

  • Interactive Coverage Maps - Visualize response time coverage based on existing stations
  • Gap Analysis - Identify areas with inadequate emergency service coverage
  • Location Optimization - Receive data-driven suggestions for optimal new station locations
  • Response Standard Integration - Analyze coverage based on NFPA 1710/1720 standards
  • Population Weighting - Factor population density into coverage analysis
  • Incident Density Analysis - Incorporate historical incident data for demand-based planning
  • Station Management - Add, modify, or remove stations to test different scenarios
  • Customizable Parameters - Set response time targets, travel speeds, and turnout times
  • Export Capabilities - Save results in multiple formats for reports and presentations

Getting Started

To begin using the Coverage Gap Finder:

  1. Navigate to the Coverage Gap Finder from the main menu
  2. Set your jurisdiction boundary using one of these methods:
    • Click "Draw Boundary" to manually outline your service area
    • Click "Upload Boundary" to import a GeoJSON file of your jurisdiction
  3. Add station locations using one of these methods:
    • Upload a CSV or Excel file containing station data
    • Manually add stations by entering coordinates or addresses
    • Click directly on the map to place stations
  4. Set coverage parameters (response times, travel speeds, etc.)
  5. Click "Calculate Coverage" to generate the analysis

Tip: For the most accurate results, use actual station locations and real response data from your jurisdiction. The system will automatically geocode addresses if you don't have coordinates.

Data Requirements

For optimal analysis, you should provide:

  • Station Data (Required) - Include at minimum:
    • Station name or ID
    • Location (address or latitude/longitude coordinates)
  • Incident Data (Optional but Recommended) - Historical emergency calls including:
    • Incident location (address or coordinates)
    • Incident date and time
    • Incident type (optional but useful for filtering)
  • Jurisdiction Boundary (Recommended) - The specific area your agency is responsible for serving

Note: If you don't have incident data, the tool will still function but will rely more heavily on population distribution for gap analysis. For the most accurate results, use at least 6-12 months of incident data.

Setting Coverage Parameters

The Coverage Gap Finder allows you to customize various parameters to match your agency's operational characteristics:

Response Standards

Select from predefined standards or set custom values:

  • NFPA 1710 - Urban career departments (4-minute travel time)
  • NFPA 1720 Urban - Population density >1000 people/mi² (9-minute response time)
  • NFPA 1720 Suburban - 500-1000 people/mi² (10-minute response time)
  • NFPA 1720 Rural - <500 people/mi² (14-minute response time)
  • NFPA 1720 Remote - Travel distance >8 miles (80% within 4-minute travel)
  • Custom - Set your own response time targets

Travel Speeds

Choose from preset travel speed options or set custom values:

  • Urban (Slow) - 20 mph average
  • Urban (Medium) - 25 mph average
  • Urban (Fast) - 30 mph average
  • Suburban - 35 mph average
  • Rural - 45 mph average
  • Highway - 55+ mph average
  • Custom - Set your own speed value

Turnout Times

Set the time from alarm to when units begin travel:

  • 0.5 minutes - 30 seconds
  • 1.0 minutes - 60 seconds (default)
  • 1.5 minutes - 90 seconds
  • 2.0 minutes - 120 seconds
  • Custom - Set your own turnout time

Tip: Different incident types may have different turnout time requirements. For example, NFPA 1710 recommends 60 seconds for EMS calls and 80 seconds for fire calls. Choose the value most appropriate for your analysis.

Understanding the Results

After analyzing your data, the Coverage Gap Finder displays several key metrics:

Coverage Metrics

  • Area Coverage - Percentage of your jurisdiction reachable within the target response time
  • Population Coverage - Percentage of population served within the target response time
  • Incident Coverage - Percentage of historical incidents that would have been reachable within the target time
  • Average Response Time - The calculated average response time across your jurisdiction

Map Visualization

The map display shows:

  • Green Areas - Locations reachable within your target response time
  • Red Areas - Coverage gaps (locations not reachable within your target response time)
  • Blue Markers - Existing station locations
  • Orange Markers - Suggested optimized station locations
  • Intensity Overlay - Optional heat maps showing population density or incident concentration

Station List

The station list table shows detailed statistics for each station, including:

  • Station name and location
  • Coverage area in square miles/kilometers
  • Population covered by each station
  • Incidents covered (if incident data was provided)

Optimization Features

The Coverage Gap Finder can suggest optimal locations for new stations based on your coverage gaps:

  1. Set Optimization Target:
    • Population Coverage - Prioritize areas with high population density
    • Area Coverage - Prioritize maximizing geographical coverage
    • Incident Coverage - Prioritize areas with high incident frequency
    • Balanced Approach - Consider all factors equally
  2. Set Number of New Stations - Specify how many new station locations to suggest
  3. Run Optimization - Click "Suggest New Locations" to generate recommendations
  4. Review Results - Examine how the suggested locations would improve your coverage metrics
  5. Apply Suggestions - Add the suggested stations to your map for further analysis

Note: The optimization algorithm uses a complex analysis of uncovered areas, call density, population distribution, and travel networks to determine the most effective new station placements.

Exporting Results

You can export your analysis results in several formats:

  • PDF Report - Complete analysis with maps, statistics, and recommendations
  • Image Export - High-resolution map image for presentations
  • Data Export - CSV or Excel file with all metrics and station data

To export your results:

  1. Run your coverage analysis
  2. Select the desired export format from the Export Results section
  3. For PDF exports, you can customize the report title and included content
  4. For image exports, you can select the desired resolution and format
  5. For data exports, you can choose which metrics to include

Best Practices

Follow these recommendations for the most effective use of the Coverage Gap Finder:

  • Use Real Data - Whenever possible, use actual incident data rather than estimates
  • Consider Time Patterns - Analyze different time periods separately (e.g., day vs. night, weekday vs. weekend)
  • Start Simple - Begin with your existing stations before adding optimization scenarios
  • Test Multiple Scenarios - Try different parameter settings to see how they affect coverage
  • Include Traffic Patterns - Adjust travel speeds to reflect actual traffic conditions
  • Focus on Critical Infrastructure - Pay special attention to coverage for hospitals, schools, senior centers, and industrial facilities
  • Combine with Other Tools - Use results alongside the Isochrone Map and Call Density Heatmap for comprehensive planning
  • Update Regularly - Re-run analyses as new incident data becomes available or community demographics change

Tip: When planning new stations, consider running analyses for different types of equipment separately. For example, you might need different coverage patterns for EMS units versus specialized apparatus like ladder trucks.

Troubleshooting

Common Issues & Solutions

Issue: Unable to log in

Solution:

  • Verify your email address and password
  • Check if Caps Lock is enabled
  • Clear browser cache and cookies
  • Use the "Forgot Password" link to reset your password
  • Contact your department administrator if issues persist

Issue: Maps not loading properly

Solution:

  • Check your internet connection
  • Ensure your browser is up to date
  • Clear browser cache
  • Disable browser extensions that might be interfering
  • Try a different browser

Issue: Unable to save incident reports

Solution:

  • Check that all required fields are completed
  • Look for error messages indicating specific issues
  • Verify your user permissions
  • Check your internet connection
  • Try saving as a draft first

Issue: Data not displaying in reports

Solution:

  • Verify that data exists for the selected time period
  • Check filter settings that might be excluding data
  • Clear browser cache
  • Verify that you have permission to view the data
  • Try a different browser

Error Messages

Common error messages and their solutions:

Error: "Access Denied"

Solution: Your user account doesn't have permission to access this feature. Contact your department administrator to request access.

Error: "Session Expired"

Solution: Your login session has timed out for security reasons. Log in again to continue.

Error: "Invalid Data Format"

Solution: The data you entered doesn't match the expected format. Check for specific requirements (e.g., date formats, number ranges).

Error: "Server Connection Lost"

Solution: Check your internet connection. If connected, the server might be temporarily unavailable. Try again later.

Performance Optimization

If you're experiencing slow performance:

  • Close other browser tabs and applications to free up system resources
  • Clear browser cache and cookies
  • Use Chrome or Firefox for best performance
  • Limit date ranges for large data queries
  • Disable browser extensions
  • Ensure your device meets the minimum system requirements

Tip: For analyzing large datasets, use the data filtering options to narrow results before generating reports and visualizations.
